Conclusion

Compared to everyday writing, biology research articles are denser and harder to read and write. This is the case not only for biology, but for most of the professional academia. Biology articles are difficult to understand and compose for a reason; they carry a large amount of specialized information in limited pages, allowing maximum efficiency for the readers. In writing a research article, therefore, conciseness and clearness are the key. Succint and clear paper cannot be written overnight, however. Writing a good biology article is a holistic process involving careful and thorough planning of the research, analysis of data, and of course, multiple rounds of updating and proofreading. Only repeatedly reading and writing biology articles will make one a good researcher; practice makes perfect.
