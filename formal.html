Executive Summary

Scientific journal articles are different from everyday news articles or personal journal entries in that they are the results of many rigorous experiment data analysis. Most journal articles take months, sometimes years to write, and much planning is needed even before the actual research data collection. For example, if you were to submit and publish an article about the efficacy of a new cancer treatment in an oncology journal, you would first plan the research, conduct it, collect data, analyze them and then start writing the article. This essay aims to scrutinize the process of academic communications among professional researchers by analyzing two real life articles, one from a scientific journal and the other from a trade publication, and by interviewing experts in the field who have been conducting researches and publishing articles for years.
